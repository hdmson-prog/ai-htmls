Nocturne Commerce

A premium ecommerce redesign for a luxury goods retailer that needed a sharper visual hierarchy, faster shopping flow, and stronger conversion signals without losing editorial polish.

See Results Back to Archive
Client Luma House
Services Web Design, Development, CRO
Timeline 10 Weeks
Impact +34% Revenue Lift
Challenge

The store felt expensive, but the buying path did not.

The original experience leaned heavily on atmosphere, but product discovery was slow and the catalog lacked a clear hierarchy. High-value products were visually buried, mobile users had to work too hard, and merchandising had little room to breathe.

We kept the brand’s editorial tone and rebuilt the commerce logic around browsing, scanning, and decision-making. The goal was to feel quieter, more deliberate, and more persuasive at every step.

Approach

A measured visual system with stronger product rhythm.

01

Sharper product hierarchy

We introduced larger product cards, stronger price signaling, and a more deliberate order of information to reduce scan time.

02

Editorial commerce modules

Feature rails, editorial pairings, and limited-edition callouts made the collection feel curated rather than crowded.

03

Conversion-focused flow

We tightened the path from collection to checkout, removing unnecessary stops and clarifying shipping and returns earlier.

Results

The redesigned path did the job without shouting.

Project summary

Luxury commerce with stronger momentum.

A complete refresh of information architecture, landing page rhythm, and commerce microcopy.

Deliverables

Outcome

A more refined storefront that preserved the premium brand feel while making the purchase path more obvious.

Origins

Where it began

In 1897, our founder arrived in the Dimbula Valley with little more than conviction and a deep reverence for the leaf. What began as a modest processing shed has grown into one of the world's most respected tea manufactories.

The estate has remained in family stewardship across four generations. Each custodian has honored the same principle: that exceptional tea requires time, attention, and an unwillingness to compromise.

We have survived wars, economic upheavals, and shifting markets by remaining focused on what matters—quality, consistency, and relationships built on trust.

Philosophy

What guides us

01

Patience over speed

Exceptional tea cannot be rushed. We reject shortcuts and honor time-tested methods that prioritize quality above efficiency.

02

Stewardship over extraction

We view ourselves as custodians, not owners. Our responsibility extends to the land, the workers, and future generations.

03

Restraint over excess

True luxury is found in refinement, not abundance. We pursue perfection in simplicity rather than complexity.

04

Knowledge over novelty

Innovation must serve tradition. We embrace new tools only when they honor established wisdom and improve outcomes.

05

Relationships over transactions

We build partnerships measured in decades. Success is defined by mutual trust and shared commitment to excellence.

Integrity over image

Our reputation is built on actions, not marketing. We let the tea speak for itself and earn trust through consistency.

Location

The Dimbula Valley

Our estate sits at elevations between 1,200 and 1,800 meters in Sri Lanka's renowned Dimbula region. Cool temperatures, consistent rainfall, and well-draining soil create ideal conditions for premium tea cultivation.

The estate spans 240 hectares, with 180 under active cultivation. We maintain biodiversity corridors, native forest reserves, and water catchment areas. The land is not simply a resource—it is a responsibility.

Our processing facility is located on-estate, ensuring leaves reach production within hours of plucking. This proximity is essential for maintaining the freshness and quality that define our teas.

Estate Reserve Black

Our flagship offering for over a century

Full-bodied orthodox black tea with malty depth and copper liquor. A testament to traditional processing methods refined across generations.

Sourced from our estate gardens in the Dimbula Valley, this tea represents the pinnacle of Ceylon black tea craftsmanship. Each leaf is hand-plucked at optimal maturity, withered with precision, and oxidized to exact specifications.

The result is a robust, satisfying cup with notes of caramel, dried fruit, and a subtle earthiness. Excellent on its own or with milk. Particularly suited to morning service.

Serving suggestions

This tea excels when brewed strong and served with or without milk. For traditional British-style service, warm the pot beforehand and use freshly boiled water. The robust character stands up well to milk and sugar, though connoisseurs may prefer it plain to appreciate the nuanced malty sweetness.

Sensory Profile

Tasting notes

Appearance

Dry leaf: Dark brown-black with consistent grade. Liquor: Deep copper-red with clarity.

Aroma

Malty sweetness with hints of dried fruit, caramel, and subtle wood notes. Rich and inviting.

Flavor

Full-bodied and robust. Initial malty sweetness followed by brisk astringency. Notes of molasses, raisin, and dark chocolate.

Finish

Clean, satisfying finish with lingering sweetness. No bitterness when properly brewed.

Heritage

A century of refinement

Estate Reserve Black has been our flagship offering since 1897. The original formulation was developed by our founder, who spent three decades perfecting the art of Ceylon black tea production.

Each year's harvest is evaluated by our master tasters to ensure consistency with this historic standard. The orthodox processing method—involving precise withering, rolling, oxidation, and firing—has remained unchanged for over a century.

This tea has been served in luxury hotels across five continents, featured in royal households, and remains a cornerstone of our identity. It represents not what is fashionable, but what endures.

The Craft

From Leaf to Liquor —
Every Step Intentional

👐 Hand-Picking — First Light Harvest
01 / Harvest

The Two-Finger Pick

Only the bud and two youngest leaves are taken — a standard known in Zhejiang as er qian yi bao. This selective picking, done before 9am when dew still sits on the canopy, ensures the most concentrated flavour and the freshest possible green.

🌬 Withering — Bamboo Tray Drying
02 / Wither & Fix

The Art of Arrest

Within hours of picking, leaves are spread on bamboo trays to wither gently in mountain air. They are then pan-fired or steamed — a critical fixation step that arrests oxidation, locking in the leaf's jade colour and grassy-sweet aroma forever.

🔄 Rolling — Gunpowder Formation
03 / Shape & Roll

Patience in Every Turn

For Chunmee, leaves are rolled lengthwise into their characteristic eyebrow shape. For Gunpowder, they are tumbled into tight pellets in heated woks — each rotation tightening the leaf, concentrating the oils and preserving freshness for months of transport.

🔍 Sorting — Hand Quality Control
04 / Sort & Grade

Nothing Passes Unexamined

Each lot is hand-sorted by experienced graders who reject broken, discoloured, or substandard pieces by sight and touch. Only material that meets Verdant House's export grade proceeds to packaging — a step that cannot be automated and will never be.

Milan Design Week: Aetheria
Unveils the Future of Steam

IMAGE_LOCKED: EXHIBITION_MILAN_DESIGN_WEEK_HERO

At the intersection of molecular gastronomy and architectural home automation, Aetheria presented a vision of the professional kitchen that transcends mere utility. This year's Milan Design Week served as the stage for our most ambitious technological leap yet: the Sub-Surface Steam Matrix.

The centerpiece of our installation at the Salone del Mobile was not just a product, but a philosophy. As residential spaces continue to shrink and functional expectations grow, the need for surgical precision in air management and thermal flux becomes paramount.

"True innovation in the kitchen isn't about adding features—it's about removing friction. The future is invisible technology working at 100% saturation."

The Physics of Precision

Our engineering leads demonstrated the new 600-series induction platform, which utilizes aerospace-grade titanium coils to maintain temperature within a 0.1-degree variance. This level of stability was previously reserved for professional laboratory environments, yet it now sits beneath a seamless sheet of volcanic satin glass.

The feedback from the global architectural community was unanimous: the "Functional Silence" achieved by our new CFD-optimized extraction units is a game-changer for open-concept floor plans.

Haptic Intelligence

Aetheria Connect 2.0 also made its debut, showcasing a predictive interface that learns from the chef's patterns. By synchronizing the preservation modules with the thermal systems, the ecosystem automatically optimizes ingredient longevity based on upcoming planned menus.

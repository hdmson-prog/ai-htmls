Brewing & Storage

For most green teas, we recommend water at 75–80°C (167–176°F). Water that is too hot will result in bitterness. For delicate teas like Gyokuro, you can use slightly cooler water at 60–65°C (140–149°F). Always let boiled water cool for 2–3 minutes before pouring.

Green teas typically steep for 1–3 minutes. First infusions are often shorter (30–60 seconds), with subsequent infusions lasting longer. Premium teas like Gyokuro can be steeped multiple times — up to 4–5 infusions, with each steep longer than the last.

Store tea in a cool, dark, and dry place, away from strong odors. Airtight containers (preferably with minimal oxygen) work best. Avoid refrigeration for most teas as moisture can affect the leaves. For Pu-erh, some collectors prefer to age it in controlled environments.

A general guideline is 3–5 grams of tea per 200ml of water. For premium teas, you may use slightly more (5–7g) to appreciate the full flavor profile. Adjust according to your personal preference — stronger or weaker as desired.

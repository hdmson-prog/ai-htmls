Hands that
know
the leaf.

First Flush · Spring

"One bud,
two leaves —
nothing more."

Our farmers harvest by hand during the first flush of spring — a narrow window of days when the new shoots carry the highest concentration of amino acids and natural sweetness.

No machine can replicate this discernment. The selection happens in the field, one cluster at a time, by families who have read these plants for their entire lives.

03

Craft as
ceremony.

Between leaf and cup lies a process of precise attention — each step a form of respect for the raw material.

Withering · 12–18 hrs

Withering

Freshly picked leaves are laid on bamboo trays in shaded air. Moisture slowly releases. The leaf softens, concentrating flavour.

De-enzyming

Brief exposure to high heat arrests oxidation — preserving the vivid green chlorophyll and delicate vegetal sweetness unique to green tea.

Pan-firing · Traditional Wok
Rolling · Shape & Release

Rolling & Shaping

Hand-rolling breaks the cell walls gently, releasing essential oils. Each tea's characteristic form — needle, spiral, flat — is created here.

Final Drying

Low, slow heat over charcoal draws out residual moisture. The tea is sealed — its flavour, aroma, and character locked within the leaf.

Drying · Charcoal Kiln

I. Origin

Rooted in
ancient earth,
shaped by cloud.

At elevations where cloud and stone meet, our teas emerge from soils cultivated for over three centuries. The altitude draws moisture, the mist diffuses light, and the mineral-rich earth imparts a clarity of flavour unachievable in the valley below.

MŪSHA works exclusively with three mountain estates — each a distinct terroir, each a distinct expression of the land's quiet intelligence.

II. Harvest

Hands that
know the
single bud.

Each spring, master pickers ascend the mountain before the dew lifts. The first flush — two leaves and a bud — is selected by hand, one stem at a time. No machine can replicate the sensitivity of a trained touch distinguishing the perfect from the nearly perfect.

III. Craft

The art of
controlled
transformation.

Between leaf and cup lies a sequence of gestures refined across generations — each step calibrated not by instrument alone, but by decades of sensory memory.

Withering · Natural Air
01

Withering

Fresh leaves rest on bamboo trays in cool, circulating air. Moisture releases slowly, the leaf relaxes — chemistry begins its quiet work.

Sha Qing · Kill-Green Fixation
02

Fixation

High heat arrests oxidation and sets the leaf's character. The master's hand navigates the wok by memory — every gesture precise, unhurried.

Rolling · Shape & Structure
03

Rolling

Gentle pressure coaxes the leaf into its final form. Cell walls open slightly, releasing essential oils that will bloom in the cup.

Final Drying · Fire & Air
04

Final Drying

A final reduction of moisture locks in flavour, aroma, and longevity. The leaf is now complete — ready for its next transformation in your cup.
